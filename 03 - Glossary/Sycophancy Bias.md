# Sycophancy Bias

The judge shifts its verdict to agree with whatever opinion is stated in the prompt. Tell it you think response A is better, or push back on its score, and it folds.

**Why it matters:** Anything in your judge prompt that hints at a preferred answer leaks into the result. Keep the prompt neutral, avoid feeding in prior scores, and never let the judge see who authored which response.

**Where it came up:** [[04 LLM as Judge - Arkadip Basu]]

**Source:** [Justice or Prejudice, arXiv:2410.02736](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02736)
